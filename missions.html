Our Missions

SpaceX's mission objectives are centered on advancing humanity's ability to live and work in space, and their Dragon and Starship vehicles are key to this multi-destination strategy.

Discover More

Earth Orbit

SpaceX utilizes both Dragon and Starship for missions in low-Earth orbit (LEO), primarily serving as a platform for conducting vital scientific research and technology development. These flights also deploy the Starlink satellite network and enable commercial human spaceflight, focusing on data collection to prepare for deep space travel and enhance life on Earth.

Orbit Frequency Every 90 minutes
Mission Duration 3 - 6 days
Altitude 300 - 500 km
Seating 2 - 4 passengers
Cupola 46” diameter / 360° views
Cargo / Science Up to 192 kg cargo

Space Station

SpaceX's Dragon spacecraft is crucial for supporting the International Space Station (ISS), reliably transporting both cargo and crew under NASA's Commercial Crew Program. As the only currently operational vehicle capable of returning significant pressurized cargo, Dragon ensures the continuation of the ISS's scientific mission by bringing back critical research results.

Orbit Frequency Every 90 minutes
Mission Duration 10 days
Altitude ~400 km
Seating 4 passengers
Cargo / Science Up to 192 kg cargo

Moon

The Moon mission objective centers on the development of the Starship as the Human Landing System for NASA’s Artemis Program. Starship is designed to return astronauts to the lunar surface and deliver the massive cargo necessary to establish a permanent, sustainable base. These missions mark a crucial step in preparing for subsequent interplanetary travel.

Orbit Frequency Every 90 minutes
Mission Duration 3 - 6 days
Altitude 300 - 500 km
Seating 2 - 4 passengers
Cupola 46” diameter / 360° views
Cargo / Science Up to 192 kg cargo

Mars

The long-term vision is the colonization of Mars, entirely dependent on the large-scale capabilities of the fully reusable Starship vehicle. SpaceX plans to send initial cargo and exploration missions to the planet to prepare for human arrivals, with the ultimate goal being the transport of large numbers of people and millions of tons of cargo to build a self-sustaining Martian civilization.

Day Length 24 hrs 37 min
Force of gravity 38% of Earth
Avg Distance From Earth 225 Mkm / 140 Mmi
